## How Are Traditional Houses in Es Mercadal Centre Rehabilitated?

The urban core of Es Mercadal preserves traditional Menorcan houses from the 18th-19th centuries, with marés and stone walls, timber-beam slabs and white-washed façades. Rehabilitation for residential use, agrotourism, boutique hotel or commercial use is a constant demand.

### Wall linings over marés and stone walls

Traditional houses have 40-60 cm thick marés or limestone walls. These offer exceptional thermal mass but uneven surfaces with no complementary insulation.

A **self-supporting drywall wall lining** (Placo or Knauf board with galvanised framing) is the most respectful intervention: an independent frame with no fixings to the wall, insulation in the cavity and board as the finish. It does not drill into or modify the original wall.

Price: **€35-50/m² installed**.

### Interior rework without touching load-bearing walls

Traditional Es Mercadal houses have small rooms, long corridors and few open areas. Drywall partitions open up kitchens to living rooms and convert former service rooms into modern bathrooms and en-suites, without touching the perimeter load-bearing walls.

The advantage over brickwork is threefold: no added weight on timber slabs that may be compromised, dry execution without moisture affecting the beams, and faster programme.

### False ceilings on upper floors

Upper floors with sloped roof profiles are levelled with a drywall false ceiling at the desired height, hiding the structure and housing thermal insulation (critical for summer comfort).

## Fornells: Restaurants, Hotels and Port Houses

**Fornells** is the municipality's most iconic coastal core: a natural harbour sheltered from the tramuntana with a reputed gastronomic offer (especially lobster stew) and a property mix of traditional fishermen's houses and modern holiday homes.

### Restaurants and hospitality

Fornells harbour restaurants have intense seasons from Easter to October and close or slow down in winter. The renovation window is therefore November to February. Typical interventions include acoustic false ceilings, fire-rated partitions for kitchen compartmentation (CTE DB-SI compliance) and moisture-resistant wall linings in wash-down areas.

### Traditional port houses

Fornells' old town has white houses with marés walls and the low ceilings typical of Menorcan coastal architecture. Salt humidity is a constant challenge: in homes less than 300 m from the sea, we use reinforced galvanised framing and H-type (moisture-resistant) board even in dry rooms to avoid premature corrosion.

## Son Parc: Villas and Golf Course

**Son Parc** is the largest residential-tourist urbanisation of the municipality, with Menorca's only 18-hole golf course and a high concentration of single-family villas, many owned by British, German and central-European buyers.

### Full villa renovations

1980s-2000s Son Parc villas now demand updating: LED false ceilings, thermal wall linings on south-facing façades to cut air conditioning, rework (merging small bedrooms into master en-suites) and acoustic insulation between semi-detached units.

Works are usually run in winter, when owners are not using the second home, and we complete projects in 3-4 weeks for 150-200 m² villas.

### Upper-mid-range finishes

In villas with generous budgets, we install false ceilings with curved cuts, perimeter LED strips, indirect-light coves and hidden cornices — finishes that set a premium renovation apart from a standard one.

## Arenal d'en Castell: Apartments and Seafront

**Arenal d'en Castell** is a tourist urbanisation with 1970s-80s apartment blocks on the first and second coast lines. The stock is strongly oriented to holiday rental.

### Holiday-apartment renovations

Renovating holiday apartments is a constant demand in Arenal d'en Castell. These 40-50 year-old apartments need a comprehensive update: rework (from 3 small bedrooms to 2 larger ones with en-suite), LED false ceilings, modernised bathrooms with moisture-resistant board and acoustic upgrades between units.

More in [drywall for holiday apartments in Menorca](/en/pladur-apartamentos-turisticos-menorca).

### Acoustic insulation in apartment buildings

Tourist blocks in Arenal d'en Castell have significant acoustic deficiencies. Single hollow-brick party walls give very little insulation between units, creating friction between permanent residents and tourists with different schedules.

Acoustic drywall wall linings with high-density mineral wool are the most efficient solution. An upgraded system (double board + 60 kg/m³ mineral wool + anti-vibration band) can reach 45-55 dB reduction, comfortably above the 50 dBA minimum in CTE-HR. ## Platges de Fornells and Na Macaret: Second Homes

Platges de Fornells and Na Macaret are two smaller residential urbanisations within the municipality, with villas and apartments mainly used as second homes. The most common renovations are interior updates (false ceilings, rework) and thermal/acoustic insulation upgrades, especially in homes built before CTE came into force in 2006.

## Factors That Affect Cost in Es Mercadal

### Central, well-connected location

Es Mercadal sits at the geographic centre of Menorca: **22 km from Maó** and **25 km from Ciutadella** via the Me-1 motorway — one of the best-connected areas on the island. The corresponding logistics cost is reflected transparently within the fixed quote.

### Old-town accessibility

Narrow streets in Es Mercadal old town and Fornells village make cargo-vehicle access difficult. Drywall boards at 1.20 × 2.60 m require minimum access; in some cases, manual lifting up narrow staircases is needed, which raises time and cost.

### Seasonal availability

Es Mercadal has strong tourist seasonality on its coastal cores. Demand concentrates between October and March, when apartments, hotels and restaurants in Fornells and Arenal d'en Castell are out of season. Capacity can tighten in these months, so plan 3-6 months ahead.

## Real Case in Es Mercadal: 110 m² Single-Family Home in the Centre

A family living in Es Mercadal centre bought a 110 m² 1970s home with original layout: 4 small bedrooms, long narrow corridor, closed kitchen and deteriorated plaster ceilings. Interior walls of hollow brick without insulation and block façades without lining, with **E energy certificate rating**.

**Goal:** rework the interior (3 generous bedrooms + kitchen open to living room), improve insulation and renew ceilings with LED.

**Work carried out:**

- **Rework**: demolition of 3 old partitions and building of 4 new drywall partitions.
- **Thermal insulation** on the 2 main façades (48 m²): self-supporting wall lining with 50 mm rockwool (40 kg/m³) and 15 mm Placo board.
- **LED false ceilings** across the whole home (110 m²): flat with 48 recessed downlights and perimeter LED strip in the living room.
- **Acoustic insulation** on master-bedroom party wall (14 m²): double board and high-density mineral wool.

**Results:**

- Drywall-line duration: **10 working days** (2 installers)
- Cost: **€9,800** (material and labour)
- Energy certificate: from **E to C rating**
- Estimated cooling/heating savings: **35-40%**
- Outdated, dark layout turned into a modern, efficient space
