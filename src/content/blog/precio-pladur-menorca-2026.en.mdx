### What is included in these prices?

A drywall installer's price per m² includes as standard:

Not included: decorative paint (€4-8/m²), tiling, new electrical works, plumbing, decorative plaster or debris management from prior demolition.

## What Factors Affect Drywall Price?

Price varies 30-60% between the minimum and maximum of each system across six measurable factors.

### Board type used

Standard type A board is the cheapest. Moisture-resistant type H board (Glasroc H, Knauf Aquapanel) for bathrooms and kitchens adds 20-30%. High-hardness type I or D board (Knauf Diamant, Placo Habito) for hotels and high-traffic premises adds 25-40%. Fire-rated type F board (EI 30, 60 or 90 minutes) is the most expensive in the catalogue. High-density acoustic board (Placo Silence, Knauf Silentboard, 15 kg/m² vs 9 kg/m² standard) also carries a premium.

### Total project area

Small projects (under 20-30 m²) work out proportionally more expensive because fixed costs of travel, setup and breakdown are spread over fewer metres. From 50-60 m² onwards, prices sit in the lower-middle band of the table.

### Design complexity

A plain continuous false ceiling is the cheapest solution. Stepped levels, cornices, arches, downlight cut-outs, integrated LED lighting or curved shapes multiply labour by 25-60%. Long straight partitions run 20% cheaper per m² than runs with many corners or direction changes.

### Site accessibility

Ground-floor work with direct access is cheaper than top-floor with no lift. In Menorca, many buildings in Maó's Historic Centre and inland Menorca lack a lift or have narrow staircases; standard boards are 2.60 m long, and manual haulage adds 10-20% to the cost.

### Existing or integrated services

Installing drywall in an empty shell is simpler than around pipes, HVAC ducts or existing wiring. If new services must be routed inside the frame, cost rises 8-15%.

### Condition of existing surfaces

Ceilings and walls with deviation greater than 2 cm force additional levelling structures (adjustable hangers, double furring) and push the price up 10-20%.

## Why is Drywall More Expensive in Menorca Than on the Mainland?

Drywall in Menorca is 12-25% more expensive than on the mainland for three measurable reasons: sea freight, labour cost and seasonal demand.

### Sea freight

Plasterboards are manufactured on the mainland (Placo, Knauf and Saint-Gobain Rigips plants in Catalonia, Valencia and Madrid) and arrive in Menorca by ship from Barcelona, Valencia and Tarragona. Sea freight, port handling and onward road transport add 12-18% to the material price over the mainland benchmark.

### The labour market

Wages in Menorca exceed the mainland average, especially between May and October, when construction competes with hospitality for labour. A skilled drywall tradesman in Menorca earns 15-25% more than in inland mainland cities.

### Seasonal demand

Construction and renovation in Menorca peaks sharply between February and May (pre-tourist season) and October-November (post-summer). In those periods timescales stretch by 2-4 weeks and prices tighten 5-10% due to installer saturation.

## Drywall vs Traditional Construction: Price Comparison

A drywall partition sits at €45-52/m² installed, against €55-70/m² for a plastered brick partition. The difference is not only economic:

- **Timescale**: drywall 1-2 days ready to paint; brick 5-10 days including drying and plastering.
- **Debris**: drywall produces 1 bag of offcuts; brick, 1-2 skips of 6 m³ (additional €200-600 disposal).
- **Weight**: drywall 25-35 kg/m²; brick 100-150 kg/m² (critical on old-building slabs).
- **Integrated insulation**: drywall takes mineral wool between studs; brick needs a separate wall lining.
- **Reversibility**: drywall can be dismantled without demolition; brick cannot.

Drywall wins in virtually every interior-partition scenario. Brick is only mandatory when the wall bears very heavy structural loads or is under permanent moisture. Dig into the comparison at [drywall vs brick](/en/blog/pladur-vs-ladrillo).

## How to Get the Best Price for Your Drywall Works

### Request at least two itemised quotes

Always compare line by line (m² of false ceiling, m² of partition, joint treatment). If a quote is 20% below the rest, ask which board and framing it includes: it usually hides standard board in place of moisture-resistant or uncertified profiles.

### Define scope well before asking for a price

Specify approximate m², system type (false ceiling, partition, wall lining), floor level, lift availability and any services to respect. A vague quote leads to 10-30% upward revisions.

### Group the works

Commissioning all drywall works in one go cuts fixed costs (travel, setup) and reduces the per-m² price by 8-15% from 30-40 m² of total area.

### Consider the time of year

Avoid February-May and September-November. In June-August and January, installers have more availability and offer better terms.

### Check that the installer works in your area

An installer based in [Maó](/en/pladur-mao) may charge travel if the job is in Es Mercadal or Ciutadella. Confirm coverage before requesting the quote.
