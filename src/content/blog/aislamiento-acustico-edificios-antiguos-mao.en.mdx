Maó buildings built between 1900 and 1970 have an original Rw of 35-42 dB on party walls, far below the current CTE-HR minimum (50 dB). A Placo Silence acoustic lining on Stil F 530 chassis with Rockwool Alpharock 60 kg/m³ wool raises the insulation to 52-62 dB for €58-95/m².

If you live in an old flat in Sant Climent, Malbúger or the Historic Centre and hear conversations, footsteps or music from your neighbour, the problem is not the volume: the original wall has insufficient mass (150-250 kg/m²) and no acoustic decoupling.

## Why Old Maó Buildings Have Low Rw

Walls built between 1900 and 1970 were not designed with acoustic criteria. NBE-CA-88 only arrived in 1988 and CTE-HR (DB-HR) came into force in 2007.

### Build types and original Rw

**1900-1940 (Historic Centre, Port de Maó, Es Castell surrounds):** marés-stone masonry walls 30-40 cm. Mass 400-600 kg/m². Original Rw: **40-46 dB**. Good in mid-low frequencies, but untreated blind boxes and shafts cut real Rw to 35-40 dB.

**1940-1960 (historic Sant Climent, upper Malbúger):** mixed walls of 25-30 cm masonry plus 12 cm solid-brick partitions. Rw: **38-42 dB**. Timber or steel joist floors with ceramic infill and no absorber.

**1960-1970 (tourism boom, Trepucó, Malbúger):** single hollow-brick party walls 4-5 cm (≈65-80 kg/m²). Rw: **33-38 dB**. Pre-stressed joist and pot slab, 4 cm compression layer, no mineral wool.

For the regulatory detail, see our guide on the [CTE-HR regulations in the Balearic Islands](/en/blog/normativa-cte-hr-baleares).

### Common weak points

- **Roller blind boxes:** 8-12 dB acoustic leak below the façade's theoretical Rw.
- **Pass-through socket boxes** between dwellings: up to -5 dB per point.
- **Uninsulated downpipe shafts:** flanking transmission of sanitary noise (30-38 dBA).
- **Wall-slab junctions** with no resilient band: vibration bridge.

## Maó Neighbourhoods with the Worst Issues

### Sant Climent

Housing stock 1960-1975, reinforced concrete frame, 4 cm hollow-brick party walls (Rw 33-38 dB). Bar and restaurant density 2015-2024 yields an ambient LAeq of 58-68 dBA at night (23:00-03:00), against the 45 dBA limit of Maó's Noise By-law for mixed residential zones.

### Malbúger

1960-1980 buildings, many with ground-floor commercial units. The steep slope raises traffic noise by 3-5 dBA. Nocturnal LAeq measured in 2024: 55-62 dBA. Combined problem: weak party wall + exposed façade.

### Historic Centre

17th-19th-century buildings with 50-80 cm marés walls (original Rw 48-55 dB by sheer mass). Interior refurbishments from the 1980s-90s with hollow-brick partitions drop Rw to 38-45 dB. Heritage protection does not restrict interior solutions (lining and false ceiling).

### Trepucó, Malbúger, Port surrounds

Boom-era 1965-1980. Typical profile: concrete frame, 4-5 cm hollow brick, joist-pot slab, aluminium windows without thermal break and 4 mm glazing. Party-wall Rw: 33-38 dB. Impact L'nT,w: 72-78 dB.

## Placo/Knauf Solutions for Old Buildings

A drywall lining weighs 25-40 kg/m² versus 100-150 kg/m² for a second brick leaf: critical on old slabs with tight load limits. Dry installation, no added moisture.

### Solution 1: Placo Silence lining on Stil F 530 (party walls)

Self-supporting anti-vibration chassis Placo Stil F 530 or Knauf CW 75, with no direct contact to the existing wall. Isover Arena or Rockwool Alpharock mineral wool in the cavity. Placo Phonique or Knauf Diamant board.

**System A (moderate upgrade):** 48 mm chassis with 5 mm acoustic band. 40 mm Isover Arena (40 kg/m³). 13 mm Placo Phonique board. ΔRw: **+10-13 dB**. Thickness: 7.5 cm. **€58-68/m²**.

**System B (recommended, CTE-HR):** 70 mm Stil F 530 chassis with 5 mm EPDM band at all contacts. 60 mm Rockwool Alpharock (60 kg/m³, λ 0.035 W/m·K). Double 13 + 13 mm Placo Phonique board, staggered joints. ΔRw: **+15-18 dB** (final Rw 52-58 dB). Thickness: 10.5 cm. **€72-85/m²**.

**System C (partial box-in-box):** Double independent chassis (10 mm gap). 80 mm Rockwool Alpharock (70 kg/m³). Double Knauf Silentboard 17.5 + 12.5 mm board with 3 mm Acoustiblok viscoelastic membrane. ΔRw: **+20-24 dB** (final Rw 58-62 dB). Thickness: 16-18 cm. **€150-220/m²** (box-in-box class).

Reference tests: UNE-EN ISO 10140-2 (lab) and UNE-EN ISO 16283-1 (field).

### Solution 2: Anti-vibration false ceiling (impact noise)

Placo or Knauf ceiling suspended from the slab with Sylomer SR 18/SR 55 or Regupol Sonus Core dampers. Cavity filled with Isover Arena.

**Standard system:** Sylomer SR 18 suspension, 60 mm Isover wool, 15 mm Placo Phonique board. Airborne ΔRw: +12-15 dB. Impact ΔL'nT,w: **-15 to -18 dB**. Headroom loss: 12-15 cm. **€62-78/m²**.

**Reinforced system:** High-performance Sylomer SR 55 suspension, 80 mm Rockwool (60 kg/m³), double Knauf Silentboard 12.5 + 12.5 mm board. Airborne ΔRw: +16-20 dB. Impact ΔL'nT,w: **-18 to -22 dB**. Headroom loss: 15-18 cm. **€82-98/m²**.

More info in [drywall false ceilings in Menorca](/en/falsos-techos-pladur-menorca).

### Solution 3: Weak-point treatment

**Pass-through socket boxes:** relocate sockets onto the new board and seal the original gap with Promat PROMASEAL acoustic mastic. ΔRw: +3-8 dB per point.

**Roller blind boxes:** Isover Arena 40 mm panel + drywall inspection hatch. Cost: €90-160 per box.

**Junctions and joints:** flexible acoustic mastic, Sika Acoustic or similar. Full perimeter sealing: +2-4 dB overall.

## Real Case: Apartment in Sant Climent, 1972

### Building and flat

5-storey building, RC frame. 4 cm hollow-brick party wall (Rw 35 dB). Pre-stressed joist and pot slab. 3rd floor. Area: 72 m².

### Baseline measurements (UNE-EN ISO 16283, ENAC-accredited lab)

- Party-wall Rw: **35 dB**
- Impact L'nT,w from floor above: **76 dB**

### Executed solution

**Party wall (18 m²):** Placo Stil F 530 70 mm chassis + 5 mm EPDM band. Rockwool Alpharock 60 mm (60 kg/m³). Double Placo Phonique 13 + 13 mm. Sika Acoustic perimeter seal.

## Considerations for Old Buildings

### Slab loads

1960-1980 slabs take 100-150 kg/m² live load. A double lining weighs 28-42 kg/m² and a Knauf Silentboard false ceiling 18-24 kg/m²: comfortable margin. On timber slabs (1900-1940), check condition first.

### Floor-area loss

System B (10.5 cm) on a 3 m wall: -0.32 m². System C box-in-box (18 cm): -0.54 m². In 50-60 m² flats, weigh the space-vs-comfort trade-off.

### Ceiling height

Old Maó flats: clear height 2.50-2.75 m. Sylomer false ceiling takes 12-18 cm, leaving 2.32-2.57 m. Maó's by-law requires 2.50 m in main rooms; exceptions are allowed for acoustic refurbishment with a technical project.

### Heritage protection

Maó Town Hall's Protection Catalogue regulates façades in the Old Town. Interior interventions (lining, false ceiling) do not require additional heritage permits.
