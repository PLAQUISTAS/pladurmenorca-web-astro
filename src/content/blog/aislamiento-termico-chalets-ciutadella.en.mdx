## Why Ciutadella Villas Need Insulation

### 1985-2008 boom under NBE-CT-79

NBE-CT-79 required a façade U-value ≤1.6 W/m²K: achievable with 20 cm block façade + empty cavity. CTE-HE (2006, revised 2013 and 2019) demands U ≤0.35 W/m²K in climate zone B3 (Menorca).

Common types in Nova Cala en Bosc, Sol de Menorca and Cala Blanca:
- Façade: rendered concrete block 20 cm + empty 5 cm air cavity. Actual U: **1.6-1.8 W/m²K**.
- Roof: tiles on brick partitions, no insulation. Actual U: **2.0-2.4 W/m²K**.
- Aluminium frames without thermal break, standard 4+8+4 double glazing. Window U: **3.2-3.6 W/m²K**.

### Large glazed areas

30-40 m² of glass with U 3.2-5.7 W/m²K against walls with U 1.6-1.8. In summer, solar gain G-value 0.68-0.75 without protection: south receives 5.2 kWh/m²·day in July (PVGIS, Ciutadella).

### Critical roof (zone B3, 800 W/m² at solar noon)

Roofs receive perpendicular radiation at noon (800 W/m² in August). Without insulation, under-roof temperature exceeds 55-60°C in July. A Knauf false ceiling with Rockwool Alpharock 100 mm (U 0.28 W/m²K) divides heat transfer by 6-7.

### South and south-west orientation

Sea-view urbanisations (Cala en Bosc, Son Xoriguer, Cala Blanca): south façade receives >5 kWh/m²·day in June-August. Insulating south/west is the priority (modified solar factor fs = 0.65-0.85).

## Summer vs Winter: Asymmetric Problem

Ciutadella climate zone B3: mild winter (5-15°C, low heating demand) and intense summer (32-36°C July-August, direct solar radiation >800 W/m²).

Priorities: south/west façades (5.2 kWh/m²·day solar) and roof (perpendicular noon radiation). North has lower economic return (prioritise in a full refurbishment).
