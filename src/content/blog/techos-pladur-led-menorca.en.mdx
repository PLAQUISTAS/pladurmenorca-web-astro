## Drywall ceiling with LED typologies

### 1. Flat ceiling with recessed downlights

The cleanest, most functional design. A completely smooth Knauf or Placo drywall surface with LED downlights integrated flush with the ceiling. Most requested design in homes for its balance between looks and function.

Downlights can be **fixed** (beam to the floor) or **adjustable** (for artwork, countertops, specific zones). Colour temperature by use:

- **2700K-3000K:** living rooms, bedrooms, relaxation zones.
- **4000K:** kitchens, bathrooms, dressing rooms.
- **Tunable white (2700K-6500K):** home automation with time-based adjustment.

Price: **€47-75/m²** for the ceiling + **€45-65** per installed downlight. For a complete reference, see [drywall prices in Menorca 2026](/en/blog/precio-pladur-menorca-2026).

### 2. Ceiling with perimeter cove and backlighting

Perimeter step or coffer with a hidden flexible LED strip projecting light onto the ceiling (backlighting). Soft, enveloping indirect effect. Popular in upper-mid-range living rooms and bedrooms. Combinable with central downlights.

Price: **€45-65/m²** coved ceiling + **€15-25/lm** of installed flexible strip with driver.

### 3. Multi-level ceiling

Different ceiling heights define zones (living, dining, open kitchen) without partitioning. Each level with independent lighting. Price: **€55-80/m²** depending on complexity.

### 4. Ceiling with integrated linear light

Recessed aluminium profiles with opal diffuser housing high-power LED strips. Continuous, geometric light lines. Signature design for boutique hotels, premium retail and architectural work. Price: **€60-90/lm** of installed light line (profile + LED + diffuser).

### 5. Knauf Cleaneo + LED systems

Perforated acoustic Knauf Cleaneo board with integrated downlights and strips. Combines acoustic absorption αw 0.55-0.75 and design. Installed price from **€65/m²** plus lighting.

## Lighting design

### Downlights per room (7-10 W)

- **Living room 20-30 m²:** 10-16 points.
- **Master bedroom 12-16 m²:** 6-8 points.
- **Secondary bedroom 9-12 m²:** 4-6 points.
- **Kitchen 8-12 m²:** 5-8 points.
- **Bathroom 5-8 m²:** 3-5 points.
- **Corridor 6-10 m²:** 3-5 points.

### Rule of thirds

Perimeter downlights at **one third** of the distance between wall and centre. Rest distributed evenly. Avoids dark zones near walls and corner shadows.

### Circuits and control

A functional design separates into independent circuits:

- **Circuit 1:** general lighting (downlights).
- **Circuit 2:** indirect lighting (cove / flexible strip).
- **Circuit 3:** accent (artwork, shelving, countertop).

Each circuit with its dimmer or automation actuator for scenes (day, dinner, cinema, night).

## LED types for drywall ceilings

### Recessed downlights

65-85 mm circular cut-outs in the drywall. Integrated driver, dimmable, 30,000-50,000 hour lifespan. Typical consumption **5-10 W** (equivalent to 35-60 W incandescent). Require **CRI ≥ 95** for chromatic rendering.

### Flexible LED strips

Tapes with SMD 2835/5050 diodes soldered every 5-10 mm. Indirect in coves and perimeters. Available in warm white, neutral, cool, tunable and RGB/RGBW. Power **9-14 W/lm**. 24 V driver. PWM or 0-10 V dimmable.

### Linear LED profiles

Aluminium with opal diffuser and high-power interior strip. Uniform continuous lines. Slot cut in the drywall. Premium option for architectural design.

## Home automation: KNX, Zigbee, DALI, Wi-Fi

The drywall false ceiling is the ideal support for home-automation systems: hosts KNX bus cabling, DALI drivers, Zigbee bridges (Philips Hue, Ikea Dirigera, Aqara) and Wi-Fi actuators (Casambi, Shelly).

Typical features:

- **Preset scenes:** day, dinner, cinema, night.
- **Voice control:** Alexa, Google Home, Siri.
- **Time-based adjustment (circadian):** tunable white from 4000K daytime to 2700K at night.
- **Presence detection** for corridors and bathrooms.
- **Integration with blinds, HVAC and audio.**

## Technical considerations in Menorca

### Minimum height

Habitability rules require **2.50 m clear** in habitable rooms. A drywall false ceiling with LED drops 10-15 cm (recessed downlight), 15-25 cm with cove and backlighting, and 25-30 cm with HVAC ducts. Recommended minimum original height: 2.60-2.70 m.

In flats in central Maó with 3-4.5 m ceilings this is not a problem. In modern 2.60 m flats: opt for low-profile downlights (35-50 mm) or flexible strips without cove.

### HVAC coordination

For ducted A/C: ducts need 20-30 cm. Drywall installer and HVAC engineer must coordinate grille and diffuser positions before closing the ceiling.

### Internal bathroom ventilation

Bathrooms without a window: extractor with ducted outlet through the false ceiling to roof or façade (UNE 100012). Plan the route before installation.

### LED heat dissipation

Downlights need rear space to dissipate heat (minimum 5 cm above the body). Respect ventilation to achieve declared lifespan (30,000-50,000 hours).
