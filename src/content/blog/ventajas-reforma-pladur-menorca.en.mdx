## The Problem with Traditional Renovations

Wet works have a sequence with no shortcuts: demolition, rubble removal, brick or block partition, curing, render, plaster, drying and finishes. Weeks of work, noise, dust and the home out of use.

In Menorca, rubble management adds cost: skips with time restrictions in Maó, Ciutadella and coastal cores, and transport to the authorised landfill at the Milà facility managed by the Consell Insular. On top, neighbour impact, with complex coexistence between residents and holiday rentals.

### The five specific problems

**Time**: a brick partition takes 7-15 days to be ready to paint. **Waste**: every m² demolished generates 80-120 kg of debris. **Dust**: cutting brick impregnates the whole home. **Cost**: masonry labour, heavy materials and rubble management push up the process. **Inflexibility**: a brick wall does not move without demolition.

---

## Drywall as the Solution: Dry Build and Modularity

The **plasterboard system** (Pladur, Placo, Knauf, Lafarge) is based on a light galvanised-steel frame onto which boards are screwed. Dry build: no water, no curing, no waits.

### 6 Real Drywall Benefits in Renovation

#### 1. Speed: 40-60% less time

A 15 m² drywall partition is installed in a day. The same partition in brick requires several days including drying. An 80 m² flat renovation is completed in **1-2 weeks** with drywall vs 3-4 weeks with traditional construction.

#### 2. Waste: 80% less

Dry build generates only board offcuts vs brick debris. **RD 105/2008** compliance (construction and demolition waste management) without complex skip management.

#### 3. Light structural weight

A standard drywall partition weighs **25-35 kg/m²**; equivalent hollow brick 100-150 kg/m². In old Menorca buildings with limited slabs, this difference is decisive.

#### 4. Integrated insulation (CTE)

Inserting mineral wool, rockwool or polyurethane foam inside the frame is easy and cheap. The most efficient system for [acoustic insulation](/en/aislamiento-acustico-menorca) (DB-HR compliance) and [thermal insulation](/en/aislamiento-termico-menorca) (DB-HE). For fire protection, EI30/EI60/EI120 boards meet DB-SI.

#### 5. Modularity and reversibility

A drywall wall can be removed without damaging structure or floor. Real value in rentals and flexible spaces.

#### 6. System versatility

Partitions, false ceilings, wall linings, EI compartmentation, curved walls, niches, mouldings and supports for TV or home automation: a single system resolves everything.

## 5 Renovation Types Where Drywall Shines

### 1. Interior rework in homes

Enlarging a bedroom, creating a walk-in wardrobe, opening a corridor or adding a toilet: **2-4 days with drywall** (€45/m²) vs 2-3 weeks with traditional partitioning. More in [drywall partition installation in Menorca](/en/tabiques-pladur-menorca).

### 2. False ceilings to hide services and integrate LED

Drywall ceilings (**from €29/m²**) hide wiring, HVAC piping and plumbing, and integrate LED lighting, speakers and grilles. In flats with tall ceilings (Maó's old town) they improve efficiency by cutting the air volume to condition. See [drywall false ceilings in Menorca](/en/falsos-techos-pladur-menorca).

### 3. Acoustic insulation between homes

Common problem in apartment buildings in tourist areas (Punta Prima, Cala en Bosc, Arenal d'en Castell) and old Maó blocks. Drywall wall linings with high-density mineral wool cut impact noise 30-40% and airborne noise 8-15 dB.

### 4. Thermal insulation upgrade (from €7/m²)

Old stone or masonry homes without insulation: a wall lining with rockwool or polystyrene cuts heating and cooling demand. Menorca's B3 climate zone (mild winters, warm summers) makes façade wall linings particularly effective against summer overheating. Direct impact on the energy certificate.

### 5. Commercial premises and hospitality refurbishment

Fast refurbishment of restaurants and premises (Fornells gastronomy port, Maó harbour, Ciutadella historic core): new layouts, insulation of kitchens and noisy areas, EI30/EI60/EI120 compartmentation with specific boards (DB-SI).

## Steps for Your Drywall Renovation in Menorca

Define the outcome (more silence, more rooms, better look, less heat), not the technical solution. Take basic measurements before requesting a quote. For works above €1,000, request a quote with an on-site visit included. Ask for references from prior works. Confirm exact scope: what it includes (material, labour, clean-up, primer) and what it does not (paint, electrical, plumbing). If the building has no lift, discuss logistics with the installer before closing the deal.

## What we see on our projects

The benefit owners only discover once the job is under way is real speed. An 80-100 m² interior renovation with drywall usually closes in 1-2 weeks, against 4-8 weeks for the equivalent job in brick and plaster. But the detail that transforms the whole experience is another one: the owner can keep living in the home for most of the works. There is no mortar water and no drying times forcing an evacuation; we work in zones and the home stays habitable. By a wide margin, this is the comment we hear most at the end.

The benefit almost everyone underestimates at quote stage is cleanliness: effectively zero debris, zero cement dust and zero disturbance to the building community. In communal blocks with neighbours on every side — the vast majority of the jobs we run in Maó and Ciutadella — this is a value that never shows on a price sheet, but it prevents neighbour conflicts, letters from the building president and after-hours phone calls. B2B clients running holiday apartments value it especially: a clean job means zero complaints from the rest of the owners in the building.

The benefit that only shows up 1-2 years after delivery is ease of modification. Routing a new cable, adding a socket, moving a switch, widening an opening, hanging a heavy cabinet that wasn't planned for... everything is resolved by opening the board, making the change and closing it, with no destructive works. Clients who have been through a brick renovation before notice it the day they want to change something: where before it meant chipping, carting off debris and replastering, now it is two hours of work and a patch. This reversibility is why many of them call us again a few years down the line.
