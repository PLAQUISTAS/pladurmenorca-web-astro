**Sant Lluís (7,500 population, 7-8 km from Maó)** is one of the municipalities with the highest renovation density in Menorca. The tourist market does not stop: apartments in Punta Prima and Binibeca refreshed every 2-3 years, coastal villas in **S'Algar, Alcaufar and Binissafuller**, hotels in continuous refurbishment cycles and local owners updating without a full renovation. Founded in 1760 under Louis XV during the French occupation, the village core has a unique grid layout of white-washed houses.

Drywall fits the market demands: speed (**1-2 weeks per apartment**), finish quality, integrated acoustic and thermal insulation and **80% less waste** (RD 105/2008) than traditional construction.

## Sant Lluís Specifics That Shape Renovations

### Tourist density and tight timescales

Most properties have tourist links (holiday rental, hotel, restaurant, second home). The typical renovation window is **October-March**, peaking January-April. Dry build with drywall (40-60% less time than traditional construction) is the perfect ally.

### High finish standards

Punta Prima, Binibeca and S'Algar demand premium finishes: perfect levelling, accurate angles, invisible joints and a smooth surface able to take any paint or decorative finish.

### Noise regulations in tourist areas

Sant Lluís has specific noise by-laws. Acoustic insulation with drywall (high-density wall linings + 40-60 mm mineral wool, CTE DB-HR compliance) is the most efficient solution. More in [acoustic insulation in Menorca](/en/aislamiento-acustico-menorca).

### Difficult access

Hillside urbanisations, narrow gates, 1970s apartments without lifts. Standard Placo or Knauf boards (2.60 m, 18-25 kg) sometimes need smaller format or logistical planning.

## Sant Lluís Village: Grid Layout and White Houses

The urban core of Sant Lluís is one of the few examples of 18th-century Enlightenment urbanism in Spain: perfectly orthogonal streets, one- or two-storey houses, white-washed façades and gabled Arabic-tile roofs. A municipal by-law regulates façade interventions (colours, openings, visible elements).

### Most common project types

**Wall linings over marés walls** (30-50 cm thick): self-supporting with no fixings to the original wall, insulation in the cavity and board as the finish. Price: **€35-48/m² installed**.

**Rework of interiors without touching load-bearing walls**: drywall partitions open up kitchens, create en-suites and reshape small rooms into modern living areas.

**Conversion to tourist accommodation**: several village houses are now holiday homes and small rural accommodation. Acoustic partitions with double board and high-density mineral wool are essential (CTE DB-HR compliance).

Interior drywall works, as they do not alter façade or volumes, only require a prior minor-works notice to the Town Hall. See our [works licences guide](/en/blog/licencia-obra-pladur-menorca).

## Punta Prima and Binibeca: Acoustic Insulation in Apartments

Punta Prima and Binibeca are the most densely touristic cores. The mix of residential and hospitality use and night-time activity make acoustic insulation the priority.

### The noise problem

1960s-90s buildings: thin slabs, half-brick partitions without insulation and original windows. Negative reviews about noise cut nightly rates and occupancy.

### Drywall solutions

**Party-wall lining**: decoupled frame + 40-60 mm high-density mineral wool. 8-12 dB improvement between apartments.

**Insulated false ceiling**: elastic suspension + mineral wool in cavity. Cuts impact noise 10-20 dB.

**Façade wall lining**: improvement against outside noise (traffic, music, night activity) without replacing joinery.

Binibeca Vell, the iconic architectural ensemble designed by Antonio Sintes in the 1960s, has specific protection: any exterior change requires Town Hall approval, but interior drywall works go ahead freely as they stay hidden.

## S'Algar, Alcaufar and Binissafuller: High-End Drywall

The S'Algar-Alcaufar-Binissafuller arc concentrates Sant Lluís's highest-value residential properties. Sea-view villas, owners from the UK, Germany and central Europe and budgets several notches above average.

### Premium segment demands

**Tight tolerances**: flatness below 2 mm per linear metre, any irregularity under raking light is unacceptable.

**Integration with other systems**: home automation, underfloor heating or recessed fan-coils, distributed audio and blind automation run through drywall cavities.

**Special finishes**: textures, reliefs, panelling and integrated mouldings.

**High-performance boards**: 15 mm boards (vs 12.5 mm standard) from Placo/Knauf/Lafarge, multi-layer combining acoustic, thermal and EI30/EI60/EI120 (DB-SI compliance) performance.

## Between-Season Renovations: 40-60% Less Time than Traditional Construction

The Sant Lluís renovation calendar is set by tourism. High season May-October, renovation window November-April.

### Real speed

A 2-bedroom apartment with full ceiling renovation and a new partition is finished in **3-5 days with drywall**. The same work in traditional construction (handcrafted plaster, brick partitions) would need 15-25 days including drying. With a 4-5 month window, the difference between 5 and 20 days is renovating one property or two.

### Coordination with other trades

After plumbing and electrical, drywall creates no bottlenecks. The electrician can step in later the same day the false ceiling closes.

### Cleanliness for parallel work

In partial renovations, dry build does not impregnate living-room furniture with cement while work goes on in bedrooms.
